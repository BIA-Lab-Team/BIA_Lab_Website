Join Us

Build the Lab
With Us

The BIA Lab is new. The people who join now aren't just members — they are founders. That means real ownership over the problems we tackle, the tools we build, and the culture we create.

We are looking for people who want to derive quantitative frameworks
for cell and tissue biology — not just apply them.
Why Now

This is a rare kind of opportunity

Shape the direction

The research agenda is not fixed. Founding members have a genuine voice in which problems we pursue, which collaborations we build, and what our north star looks like in five years.

Build from scratch

We are designing the lab's infrastructure, computational frameworks, and culture from the ground up. Everything you contribute here will outlast you — and carry your fingerprints.

Move fast on hard problems

Early labs are nimble. There are no inherited systems to maintain, no established hierarchies to navigate. If you have a bold idea and the will to execute it, we will find the resources.

Who We're Looking For

Drive matters more than background

Strong mathematical intuition, a taste for large and messy imaging data, and genuine curiosity about why cells behave the way they do are more important to us than a specific prior skill set. We have had outstanding lab members from physics, engineering, mathematics, and biology — what they shared was not a field, it was a way of attacking problems.

You want to derive, not just apply. You reach for first principles when a tool doesn't exist yet.

You are drawn to hard, messy data. Terabyte-scale imaging, biological noise, and incomplete ground truth excite rather than deter you.

You care about biological meaning. The math is a means — understanding how cells encode and transmit decisions is the end.

You finish things. Ideas are easy; execution is what creates tools that thousands of researchers can use.

Culture

What we're building together

Flat hierarchy, high standards

Everyone's ideas get heard. The only currency is quality of thinking and execution. We expect a lot and give a lot back.

Open science by default

All code, data, and preprints are public from day one. Science progresses faster when knowledge is shared without friction.

Career development with intention

Regular 1-on-1s, a personal development plan tailored to where you want to go — academia, industry, or something entirely new — and warm introductions when you're ready to move.

Intellectual courage

We ask unconventional questions and take real intellectual risks. That is the only way transformative science gets done.

Postdoctoral Fellows

As one of the lab's founding postdocs, you will have an outsized hand in shaping what we work on and how we work. We are looking for someone with a research vision — someone who has been quietly frustrated by the limits of existing tools and already has ideas about what to build next.

We will give you the independence to pursue that vision, the infrastructure to move fast, and the mentorship to set you up for the career you want.

Research Engineers

The lab's software is used by researchers worldwide. As a founding engineer, you won't be maintaining someone else's legacy code — you'll be designing the architecture from scratch, making decisions that will matter for years.

We value engineers who think like scientists: people who care about why as much as how, who are willing to read a paper before reaching for a library.

Process

How to apply

We review applications on a rolling basis. Strong candidates are contacted within two weeks.

1

Read our papers and imagine what you could build here

Explore our research. Ask yourself: where could you push the needle? How could you help us reach our north star — and how could we help you grow into the scientist you want to become?

2

Prepare your materials

CV · 1-page cover letter explaining your interest and fit · 1-page personal statement describing your career goals and how training specifically in the BIA Lab will help you achieve them · research statement (max 2 pages, excluding references) outlining a research topic you could propose and execute in the BIA Lab and Vanderbilt ecosystem · contact details for 2–3 referees.

3

Submit below or email us

Use the form, or email felix.y.zhou@vanderbilt.edu with subject line [Position Name] – Application.

4

Interviews

Shortlisted candidates are invited for a 45-minute video call, followed by an on-site visit for finalists.
