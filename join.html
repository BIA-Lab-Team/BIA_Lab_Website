Culture

What we value

We are committed to building an inclusive, supportive, and scientifically rigorous environment.

🤝

Collaboration first

We work closely with experimental labs. No one succeeds alone — we share credit, code, and ideas openly.

🔓

Open science

All code, data, and preprints are public. We believe science progresses faster when knowledge is shared.

📈

Career development

Regular 1-on-1s, a personal development plan, and connections to industry and academia for your next step.

🧪

Intellectual freedom

We encourage bold ideas and support lab members in pursuing unexpected directions with appropriate resources.

🚀

Imagination

We venture beyond what is known. Asking unconventional questions and taking intellectual risks is how transformative science gets done.

💪

Tenacity

The resolve to learn whatever it takes to realise the idea and solve the problem — we push through complexity and setbacks to see the work through.

Process

How to apply

We review applications on a rolling basis. Strong candidates are contacted within two weeks.

1

Read our papers and imagine what you could build here

Explore our research. Ask yourself: where could you push the needle? How could you help us reach our north star — and how could we help you grow into the scientist you want to become?

2

Prepare your materials

CV, a 1-page cover letter explaining your interest and fit, contact details for 2–3 referees, and a research statement (max 2 pages, excluding references) outlining a research topic you could propose and execute in the BIA Lab and Vanderbilt ecosystem.

3

Submit below or email us

Use the form, or email felix.y.zhou@vanderbilt.edu with subject line [Position Name] – Application.

4

Interviews

Shortlisted candidates are invited for a 45-minute video call, followed by an on-site visit for finalists.
